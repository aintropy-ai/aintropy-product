UI / UX Plan · March 2026
Designing for
the Answer
Every design decision starts with one question: does this make the answer faster and more trustworthy?
Principle 1
Business user first
If a non-technical person cannot use it without training, it is too complex.
Principle 2
Answer is the product
Every UI decision should make the answer faster and more trustworthy.
Principle 3
Show your work
Always show how Kurious found the answer. Never just return a result.
Principle 4
Speed as a feeling
The interface should feel instant even when retrieval takes a moment.
Principle 5
Trust by design
Data privacy, source citations, and transparency should be visible — not buried in docs.

Ongoing
Pilot Structure
Before the pilot starts
  • Customer identifies 10 questions they currently can't answer quickly
  • Customer identifies 5 questions they already know the answer to (verification)
  • Customer identifies 3 cross-source questions (require connecting 2+ sources)
  • Kurious answers all verification questions correctly — non-negotiable before pilot begins
Pilot success criteria
  • Time to answer reduced by >70%
  • Answer accuracy >95% on verified queries
  • Zero hallucinations during the 2-week pilot
  • >4/5 users still actively using by end of week 2
  • Customer agrees to continue beyond pilot
What a "Pass" Looks Like
Kurious is ready for its first enterprise pilot when:
  • Hallucination rate <1% on benchmark test set
  • Recall@10 >90% on benchmark test set
  • Retrieval time <1 second on benchmark data
  • 4/5 usability session participants complete task without assistance
  • 4/5 usability session participants trust the answer
  • Red team testing complete — all critical failures fixed
  • Verification queries for first customer answered correctly

Objection Handling
"We already use ChatGPT / Copilot / Gemini." ▼
Those tools are trained on the internet. They have never seen your data. Ask them about your internal contracts, client files, or proprietary databases — they will hallucinate or say they don't know. Kurious is built specifically for the data that general AI tools cannot reach.
"What happens to our data?" ▼
Nothing. Kurious integrates into your existing systems — it reads your data where it lives. Nothing is copied, moved, or sent outside your environment. Your data is never used to train any model. We never see it.
"How long does it take to set up?" ▼
An admin can connect the first data source and have Kurious working in under a day. No migration project. No IT overhaul. Connect, ask, get answers.
"How is this different from our internal search?" ▼
Internal search finds documents. Kurious finds answers. Ask your search tool "What were the key terms in the Johnson contract from last March?" — it returns a list of documents. Ask Kurious the same question — it returns the answer, the clause, and the exact page it came from.
"What if Kurious gets it wrong?" ▼
Every answer comes with its source — the exact document, page, timestamp, or row used. You can verify any answer in one click. And our hallucination rate on benchmarked datasets is below 1%.
Positioning vs Competitors
vs Glean
Glean connects your SaaS tools — Slack, Drive, Jira, Salesforce. Kurious connects your institutional data — the contracts, databases, legacy systems, and proprietary records that Glean was never built for. Glean is for your work tools. Kurious is for your knowledge.
vs Microsoft Copilot
Copilot works inside Microsoft products. If your data is in a legal database, a government registry, a video archive, or a proprietary system — Copilot cannot reach it. Kurious can.
vs ChatGPT Enterprise
ChatGPT Enterprise lets you upload files. Kurious integrates with your live systems — not just files you upload, but databases that update in real time. And Kurious never trains on your data.
vs Building In-House
Building a RAG system in-house takes 6–12 months of engineering time and still needs to be made usable by non-technical staff. Kurious is ready to deploy in days and improves with every query.
