# AIntropy — Marketing Flywheel

> First ever marketing strategy for AIntropy. March 2026.

---

## Why a Flywheel, Not a Funnel

A funnel is one-time. You push people in at the top, some convert at the bottom, and you start over. A flywheel is self-reinforcing — every customer, every piece of content, every demo makes the next one easier. For a company at AIntropy's stage (zero marketing history, strong product, limited resources), a flywheel is the only rational choice. You cannot afford to restart from zero every quarter.

---

## The Core Insight That Drives Everything

AIntropy has one thing most AI companies never have at this stage: **a working demo on real, massive, public data.**

85 million government records. 23 agencies. 8 formats. Queryable in plain language. Verifiable against ground truth.

This is not a toy demo. This is proof. And proof is the most powerful marketing asset in B2B. Every decision in this flywheel is built around turning that proof into awareness, awareness into trust, and trust into customers.

## Phase 1 — Demo as Proof

**What:** Make the NJ Open Data demo the most visible thing AIntropy has.

**Why:** Enterprise buyers do not trust claims. They trust evidence. A live demo on 85M public records that anyone can verify is evidence. It does the sales job before a single conversation happens.

### Tactics

| Tactic | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Publish the blog | Written proof. Title: *"Your AI Has Never Seen Your Data."* Targets searches like "enterprise RAG" and "AI on private data." |
| Demo video | 3-minute walkthrough — real query, thinking steps visible, sources cited, answer verified. Post everywhere. |
| Public demo link | `aintropy.ai/nj_demo` — linkable, shareable, no sign-up required. |
| HuggingFace | ChemRAG leaderboard already live. Keep it updated. ML community watches HuggingFace daily. |

**Rationale:** You are spending zero on ads. Your demo is your ad. Every view of the demo is a qualified impression — nobody watches a RAG demo unless they care about RAG.

## Phase 2 — Content and Distribution

**What:** Turn the demo and the thesis into content distributed across the right channels.

**Why:** AIntropy's buyers (CTOs, VPs of AI, enterprise AI engineers) live on LinkedIn and read technical newsletters. That is where you need to be.

### LinkedIn — Highest Priority

- **Who posts:** Kunal and Nirmit personally. Company page alone is not enough — people follow people, not logos.
- **Frequency:** 3x per week minimum for the first 2 months.

**What to post:**

- The **"perception vs seeing" thesis** — Kunal's original insight that AI can *see* data but cannot *perceive* it. This is a genuinely new idea and will generate debate, shares, and followers.
- Behind-the-scenes on building Kurious — what problems they hit, what they learned
- NJ demo results — specific numbers, specific queries, specific failures of GPT / Gemini / Claude vs Kurious
- Short clips from the demo video

> **Why LinkedIn:** Enterprise decision-makers are on LinkedIn. A post that gets 20,000 impressions from the right people is worth more than 1 million impressions from the wrong ones.

### X / Twitter

- Live-post demos, share technical findings, engage with the AI research community
- Tag relevant papers, researchers, and companies when genuinely relevant

> **Why:** The AI/ML community is extremely active on X. One retweet from a known researcher can bring 10,000 developers to your demo.

### Technical Newsletters

Submit the blog to:
- **The Batch** — DeepLearning.AI
- **Import AI** — Jack Clark
- **Last Week in AI**
- **The AI Edge**

> **Why:** Earned media in a trusted newsletter is worth 10x a paid ad to this audience.

### GitHub

- `aintropy-ai` org should be active — READMEs polished, repos public where possible
- Developers check GitHub before trusting any AI company

> **Why:** GitHub is a trust signal. An empty or messy org makes engineers skeptical.

### HuggingFace

- Keep leaderboard updated, add new benchmarks as Kurious improves
- Post model cards, datasets, evaluations

> **Why:** The ML research community discovers companies through HuggingFace. It is free, permanent, and credible.

## Phase 3 — Developer Awareness

**What:** Get engineers and AI practitioners to know AIntropy exists and understand what it does.

**Why:** Developers are the most valuable early audience for Kurious. They evaluate tools, recommend them to their companies, and build on top of them. One developer who loves Kurious can bring their entire enterprise org with them.

### Tactics

- **API access or waitlist** — developers want to try things themselves, not watch demos. Even a limited API beta with a waitlist creates urgency and word-of-mouth.
- **Technical deep-dives** — blog posts explaining how Kurious works: late interaction ColBERT, hybrid RRF, multimodal indexing. This builds credibility with engineers.
- **Open source something small** — a utility, a benchmark, a dataset. Open source generates GitHub stars, stars generate awareness.
- **Discord or Slack community** — small invite-only community for early users. Creates belonging and feedback loops.

**Rationale:** Developers talk to each other constantly. If 50 engineers understand and respect what AIntropy is building, that is worth more than 50,000 LinkedIn impressions from generic content.

## Phase 4 — Inbound Interest

**What:** Convert awareness into concrete interest from potential customers.

**Why:** All the content and demo work in Phases 1–3 should generate inbound. The job of Phase 4 is to capture and convert that inbound efficiently — without a sales team.

### Tactics

- **Clear CTA on aintropy.ai** — one clear call to action above the fold: *"Bring your data. Get a demo."* with a simple form. Not a generic "Contact us."
- **"Bring Your Data" landing page** — a company describes their data problem and requests a scoped demo. Pre-qualifies leads before any human time is spent.
- **LinkedIn DMs** — when a relevant person engages with a post (likes, comments), follow up directly. One genuine message referencing what they said. Not copy-paste.
- **Track who views the public demo** — analytics on the NJ demo show what companies are visiting. Tools like Clearbit or Apollo can identify them. Warm outreach to companies who already visited converts far better than cold outreach.

**Rationale:** Inbound leads are 5–10x more likely to convert than cold outreach. Phases 1–3 exist to make Phase 4 easy.

## Phase 5 — First Three Customers

**What:** Close 3 paying customers or serious pilots in the first 6 months.

**Why:** Three is enough to generate three case studies, learn what the real pain points are, and have enough revenue signal to validate pricing and positioning.

### Who to Target First

| Segment | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Government agencies** | The NJ demo is direct proof-of-concept for their exact use case. Removes the "will this work for us" objection before it is raised. |
| **Mid-size enterprises** | Insurance, healthcare, financial services — all have massive fragmented internal data. Same problem, higher budget. |
| **AI consultancies** | Firms that build AI solutions for enterprises could resell or white-label Kurious. One consultancy could bring 10 enterprise clients. |

---

## Phase 6 — Case Studies Back Into the Flywheel

**What:** Every customer becomes a case study. Every case study becomes content.

**Why:** Social proof compounds. The first case study makes the second customer easier. After five case studies, inbound starts to accelerate on its own.

### Format for Each Case Study

- One-page PDF for sales conversations
- A blog post — the real story, specific numbers, what the customer was trying to do and what Kurious enabled
- A LinkedIn post from the customer's own team (highest-trust signal possible)
- A 2-minute video testimonial if possible

## What Not to Do

- **No paid ads yet** — ICP not defined clearly enough, no conversion flow to justify spend
- **No trying every channel at once** — LinkedIn and one newsletter is enough for the first 2 months
- **Do not wait for the product to be "perfect"** — the NJ demo works now. Ship the blog. Post the demo. Learn from real feedback.
- **No generic AI content** — "AI is transforming industries" is noise. Everything AIntropy posts must be specific, credible, and backed by real demo data.

## The One Metric That Matters Right Now

> **Demo views per week.**

Not followers. Not impressions. Not LinkedIn reactions. The number of people who actually click into the NJ demo and try a query. That is a qualified person who understood the problem enough to try the solution. Everything else is vanity until that number is consistently growing.
