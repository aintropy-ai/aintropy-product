## The Framework: Category Creation + Founder-Led Flywheel

After analysing what is actually working for B2B AI companies right now, the right framework for Kurious is not a traditional funnel. It is not PLG (product-led growth) — Kurious requires integration, not self-serve sign-up. It is not pure demand generation — the team is too small for a full campaign machine yet.

The framework that fits Kurious is **Category Creation powered by a Founder-Led Flywheel.**

Here is why.

---

## Why Category Creation

Most AI companies are competing in existing categories — AI search, RAG platforms, enterprise chat. They are fighting for attention in a crowded space where the buyer already has a mental model of what the product is.

Kurious is not an AI search tool. It is not a RAG platform. It is not an enterprise chatbot.

**Kurious is an Organisational Intelligence Engine.**

That category does not exist yet. Which means Kurious can define it, own it, and become synonymous with it — the way Salesforce owns CRM, the way HubSpot owns Inbound Marketing, the way Slack owns team communication.

When you create a category, you do not compete. You lead.

**The category name:** Organisational Intelligence

**The category problem:** Every organisation knows more than it can access. The gap between what an organisation knows and what its people can find costs billions of dollars and thousands of hours every year.

**The category solution:** An intelligence layer that makes everything an organisation knows instantly accessible to everyone who needs it.

---

## Why Founder-Led Flywheel

The flywheel is self-reinforcing. Unlike a funnel which you have to refill constantly, the flywheel builds momentum — each rotation is easier and faster than the last.

For Kurious at this stage, the flywheel is founder-led because:
- Kunal and Nirmit have the credibility and the story
- Enterprise buyers buy from people they trust, not from brand pages
- The "perception vs seeing" thesis is a genuinely new idea that only the founders can tell authentically
- Founder content outperforms company content by 10:1 on LinkedIn

## What Is Actually Working Right Now (March 2026)

Research into what B2B AI companies are doing that actually drives results:

### 1. Founder-Led LinkedIn — Highest ROI by Far
Companies like Notion, Linear, Loom, and newer AI companies like Cursor and Perplexity grew primarily because their founders posted authentically on LinkedIn and X. Not polished corporate content — real thoughts, real failures, real insights.

**What works:** Specific, opinionated posts. "Here is something I believe that most people disagree with." "Here is what we got wrong and how we fixed it." "Here is something we saw in our data that surprised us."

**What does not work:** Announcements, feature launches, generic "AI is changing everything" content.

### 2. Demo-Led Growth
Not product-led growth (which requires self-serve). Demo-led growth — a demo so compelling that sharing it is the primary acquisition channel. Loom grew through people sharing Loom recordings. Figma grew through people sharing Figma links. Kurious should grow through people sharing Kurious answers.

**The mechanism:** Every answer Kurious returns should be shareable — a link that shows the answer and the source. When a business user shares an answer with a colleague, that colleague sees Kurious working. That is the most powerful marketing possible.

### 3. Dark Social — The Most Underrated Channel
Most B2B purchasing decisions happen in conversations that are invisible to marketing analytics — Slack messages, WhatsApp chats, email forwards, private LinkedIn DMs. This is called Dark Social.

You cannot track it but you can engineer for it:
- Make content that people want to forward privately ("You need to see this")
- The NJ demo blog post is exactly this — a specific, surprising piece of evidence
- Case studies with real numbers get shared in executive Slack channels constantly

### 4. Benchmark Marketing
AI companies that publish honest benchmarks — where they test themselves against competitors on real tasks — get enormous credibility with technical buyers. Vectara, Cohere, and others have done this effectively.

Kurious should publish: "We ran 100 cross-agency queries through GPT-4o, Gemini, and Kurious. Here are the results." The NJ demo already proves this works.

### 5. Community Before Product
The companies that win in enterprise AI are building communities of practitioners before they have enterprise customers. A private community of 200 senior AI practitioners who respect what you are building is worth more than 10,000 LinkedIn followers.

## The 90-Day Marketing Plan

### Weeks 1–2: Foundation
**Goal:** Make sure the message is right before distributing it.

- [ ] Finalise Kurious positioning — "Organisational Intelligence Engine" (align with Kunal and Nirmit)
- [ ] Update aintropy.ai — Kurious gets its own section, clear value proposition, "Request pilot" CTA
- [ ] Write the category creation post — Kunal writes a 500-word LinkedIn post introducing the concept of Organisational Intelligence and why it is the next frontier
- [ ] Prepare the demo script for the first vertical (consulting firms)

**Why:** Distributing the wrong message is worse than no distribution at all. Fix the message first.

---

### Weeks 3–4: Founder Content Launches
**Goal:** Establish Kunal and Nirmit as the leading voices on Organisational Intelligence.

**Kunal posts (LinkedIn, 3x per week):**
- Post 1: The "perception vs seeing" thesis — AI can see your data but cannot perceive it. Why that distinction matters.
- Post 2: What we found when we indexed 85 million NJ government records — specific, surprising finding
- Post 3: The question nobody in AI is asking — why does every large organisation have a knowledge problem?

**Nirmit posts (LinkedIn, 2x per week):**
- Post 1: The technical reality — why RAG alone is not enough and what the intelligence layer adds
- Post 2: Behind the build — specific engineering challenge we solved and what we learned

**Why:** Two founders posting consistently and specifically will build an audience of exactly the right people — AI practitioners, enterprise technology buyers, and industry observers — in 4 weeks.

---

### Weeks 5–6: Proof Distribution
**Goal:** Get the NJ demo and blog in front of the right people.

- [ ] Publish the NJ Open Data blog (pending Kunal approval on angle)
- [ ] Submit to 3 technical newsletters: The Batch, Import AI, Last Week in AI
- [ ] Post the demo video on LinkedIn — Kunal walks through a cross-agency query, shows GPT failure, shows Kurious success
- [ ] Submit to HuggingFace — publish benchmark results

**Why:** Proof is the most powerful content. One specific, verifiable proof point in a trusted newsletter reaches 50,000 qualified readers.

---

### Weeks 7–8: First Conversations
**Goal:** Convert content momentum into actual pilot conversations.

- [ ] Identify 10 target companies in consulting and legal verticals
- [ ] Kunal or Nirmit reaches out to 2–3 people who engaged meaningfully with content
- [ ] "Bring Your Data" landing page live on aintropy.ai — specific CTA for enterprise pilot
- [ ] Track who is visiting the NJ demo — use analytics to identify companies and warm outreach

**Why:** By week 7, content has been running for 5 weeks. Warm outreach to people who have already seen the content converts at 5–10x the rate of cold outreach.

---

### Weeks 9–12: First Pilot
**Goal:** Run the first 2-week pilot with a real enterprise customer.

- [ ] Pilot running — daily monitoring, weekly check-in
- [ ] Start drafting case study during the pilot, not after
- [ ] Kunal continues posting — now with a hint that real pilots are happening
- [ ] Prepare the case study template so it is ready the moment the pilot ends

**Why:** Moving from content to pilot in 9 weeks is fast for enterprise. It is achievable if the content work in weeks 1–8 is done right and the first outreach is warm.

## What Kurious Should NOT Do Right Now

**No paid advertising.** The ICP is too narrow and the conversion flow is not ready. Every pound/dollar spent on ads before you have a case study is wasted.

**No content marketing agency.** Generic AI content will damage the brand. All content must come from people who actually understand what Kurious does.

**No spray-and-pray outreach.** 10 deeply researched, highly personalised outreach messages will outperform 1,000 generic ones. Every.single.time.

**No events or conferences yet.** The message is not locked. Spend events budget on pilots instead.

## The Long Game — Category Ownership

If Kurious executes this plan well, by month 6:

- "Organisational Intelligence" is a phrase that Kunal introduced and people are using
- Kurious has 2–3 published case studies with real numbers
- The NJ demo has been seen by tens of thousands of relevant people
- Enterprise AI buyers think of Kurious when they think of their knowledge problem
- Competitors start describing themselves in relation to Kurious — "like Kurious but for X"

That last point is the signal that category creation has worked. When competitors define themselves by reference to you, you own the category.
