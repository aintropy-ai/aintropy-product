# Kurious — Brand Strategy

> Owned by Shivangi Mishra · Draft v1 · March 2026

---

## The Name

**Kurious.** Not Curious — Kurious.

The spelling is intentional. It signals that this is not a generic AI tool. It is something new. Something that thinks differently. The K is a quiet statement — we do not follow conventions, we set them.

---

## What Kurious Is — In One Sentence

> **The organisational brain that knows everything your company knows — and finds it in seconds.**

---

## Brand Positioning

Most AI tools are built for the internet. Kurious is built for your world.

Every organisation on the planet has spent years collecting data — contracts, reports, emails, databases, videos, spreadsheets, case files. That data holds the answers to almost every question the business will ever ask. But nobody can get to it fast enough to matter.

Kurious changes that. It lives inside your organisation, reads everything you have, and gives anyone — not just engineers, not just analysts — the exact answer they need, with the source, in seconds.

No data leaves. No training on your information. No hallucinations. Just answers.

## Brand Personality

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Confident** | Never hedges. Gives answers, not summaries. Shows its work. |
| **Precise** | Every answer comes with a source. No vagueness. |
| **Fast** | Speed is a core part of the brand — "in seconds" appears everywhere |
| **Trustworthy** | "We never see your data" is said early and often |
| **Human** | Talks to business people, not engineers. Plain language always. |
| **Curious** | The name is the personality — always searching, always finding |

## Brand Voice

**Do say:**
- "Find anything. In seconds."
- "Your data stays yours."
- "The answer is in there somewhere. Kurious finds it."
- "Ask in plain language. Get the exact answer."
- "Every answer comes with its source."

**Never say:**
- "AI-powered" — everyone says this, it means nothing
- "Leverage" — corporate jargon
- "State-of-the-art" — empty claim
- "RAG" or "vector embeddings" in customer-facing copy — nobody cares
- "Synergy" — obviously

## The Tagline

**"Ask anything. Know everything."**

Simple. Human. Fast. Applies to every vertical — legal, finance, consulting, government.

Alternative options:

## Visual Direction

- **Colour:** Deep navy or near-black base. Cyan or electric blue accent — signals intelligence, precision, technology without being cold.
- **Typography:** Clean geometric sans-serif. Inter or equivalent. No decorative fonts.
- **Imagery:** Abstract data flows, not stock photos of people at laptops. Visualise the invisible — information moving, connecting, being found.
- **Motion:** Fast. Purposeful. Nothing decorative. Animations show process — retrieval, connection, answer arriving.
- **Tone of visuals:** Precise and confident. Not playful, not corporate. Somewhere between Linear and Notion — clean but with depth.

## The Brand Promise

**Kurious gives you the answer. Not a guess. Not a summary. The answer — from your own data, in your own systems, in seconds.**

That promise never changes. Every product decision, every piece of marketing, every conversation with a customer should reinforce it.
