## What Product Marketing Does for Kurious

Product marketing sits between the product and the market. It answers three questions:

1. **Who** is this for — exactly
2. **What** does it do for them — in their language, not ours
3. **Why** Kurious and not anything else

Every piece of content, every sales conversation, every demo, every onboarding email comes from the answers to these three questions.

## Who Kurious Is For — The Ideal Customer Profile

### Enterprise ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**The Organisation:**
- 500+ employees
- Has data spread across multiple systems, formats, and departments
- Has tried using general AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) and found them insufficient for internal data
- Operates in a regulated or data-sensitive environment (legal, finance, government, consulting)
- Has a budget owner who cares about team productivity and decision quality

**The Champion (person who buys):**
- Title: Head of Operations, VP of Strategy, Chief of Staff, Head of Legal, CTO, or Director of AI
- Pain: Their team wastes enormous time finding information. Decisions get delayed. They know the answer is in the data somewhere but nobody can get to it fast.
- Motivation: They want to be the person who fixed this. Internally, being the person who brought in Kurious is a career win.
- Blocker: Worried about data security, worried about adoption, worried about integration complexity

**The End User (person who uses it daily):**
- Business analyst, lawyer, financial analyst, strategy consultant, operations manager
- Not technical
- Currently uses email, SharePoint search, or asks a colleague to find information
- Frustrated by how long it takes to find things they know exist

## Objection Handling

Every enterprise sales conversation will hit the same objections. Have the answer ready before they ask.

**"We already use ChatGPT / Copilot / Gemini."**
> Those tools are trained on the internet. They have never seen your data. Ask them a question about your internal contracts, your client files, your proprietary databases — they will either hallucinate or tell you they don't know. Kurious is built specifically for the data that general AI tools cannot reach.

**"What happens to our data?"**
> Nothing. Kurious integrates into your existing systems — it reads your data where it lives. Nothing is copied, moved, or sent to any server outside your environment. Your data is never used to train any model. We never see it.

**"How long does it take to set up?"**
> An admin can connect the first data source and have Kurious working in under a day. You do not need a migration project. You do not need an IT overhaul. You connect, you ask, you get answers.

**"How is this different from our internal search?"**
> Internal search finds documents. Kurious finds answers. Ask your search tool "What were the key terms in the Johnson contract from last March?" — it will return a list of documents. Ask Kurious the same question — it will return the answer, the clause, and the exact page it came from.

**"What if Kurious gets it wrong?"**
> Every answer Kurious gives comes with its source — the exact document, page, timestamp, or row it used. You can verify any answer in one click. And our hallucination rate on benchmarked datasets is below 1%.

## Positioning Against Competitors

### Vs. Glean
Glean connects your SaaS tools — Slack, Drive, Jira, Salesforce. Kurious connects your institutional data — the contracts, databases, legacy systems, video archives, and proprietary records that Glean was never built for. Glean is for your work tools. Kurious is for your knowledge.

### Vs. Microsoft Copilot
Copilot works inside Microsoft products. If your data lives in SharePoint and Teams, Copilot is fine. If your data is in a legal database, a government registry, a video archive, or a proprietary system — Copilot cannot reach it. Kurious can.

### Vs. ChatGPT Enterprise
ChatGPT Enterprise lets you upload files. Kurious integrates with your live systems — not just files you upload, but databases that update in real time. And Kurious never trains on your data.

### Vs. Building In-House
Building a RAG system in-house takes 6–12 months of engineering time, requires ongoing maintenance, and still needs to be made usable by non-technical staff. Kurious is ready to deploy in days and improves with every query.

## The Demo Strategy

**The demo is the best marketing asset Kurious has.**

Every product marketing activity should drive toward one outcome: getting the right person to see a live demo on real data.

### Demo Principles
1. **Use their vertical** — if it is a law firm, show a legal query. Never show the NJ demo to a financial services firm. Relevance kills objections.
2. **Show failure first** — run the same query through ChatGPT or Copilot first. Let them fail. Then run it through Kurious. The contrast does the selling.
3. **Let them ask** — halfway through the demo, let the prospect type their own query. The moment they see Kurious answer their own question is the moment they buy.
4. **Show the source** — always click through to the source. This is what makes Kurious trustworthy. Never skip this step.

## Launch Sequence — First Enterprise Customer

### Before the pilot starts:
- One-pager for the champion to share internally (problem, solution, proof, privacy)
- Demo script tailored to their vertical
- Pilot proposal document — scope, success metrics, timeline

### During the pilot:
- Weekly check-in email template — usage numbers, highlights, questions
- Slack or Teams channel with the customer for real-time support

### After the pilot:
- Case study written with the customer — specific numbers, specific quotes
- LinkedIn post from the customer's team (if they agree)
- Reference call offer — they speak to the next prospect directly
